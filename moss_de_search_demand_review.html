11 · Site architecture — validating the 7-layer plan

Does the demand data support the “7 ways to dominate demand” model?

Verdict in one line. The 7-layer architecture (Products, Solutions, Workflows, Teams, Industries, Infrastructure, Integrations) is directionally right but should not be built evenly. What the data validates

The plan’s instinct — multiple structured entry points feeding one destination — matches a market where demand is fragmented across informational jobs and a few buying clusters. Products + Integrations + Solutions directly map to where intent and product fit are highest, and Workflows is the right home for the large “help me do X” and calculator demand that otherwise has no destination on the Moss site today.

Where to challenge the plan

Treating all seven layers as equal demand-capture surfaces is not supported here. Teams, Industries and Infrastructure show weak organic-search evidence in this German dataset — building them out for SEO first risks effort without pipeline. Recommend a demand-weighted rollout: prove the four high-evidence layers, instrument them, then validate Industries with dedicated vertical research before committing. Use Teams/Infrastructure mainly to aid conversion and trust, not to chase search volume.

Recommended sequencing. Phase 1: Products (cards, expense, AP) + Integrations (DATEV). Phase 2: Solutions (own “Ausgabenmanagement”) + Workflows (E-Rechnung guide hub, calculators). Phase 3: validate Industries with fresh vertical keyword research; layer Teams & Infrastructure as conversion/trust support. Measure non-brand impressions and bottom-funnel clicks per layer before expanding.

13 · Method & analytical integrity

What the data can and cannot tell us

Sources

Keyword universe: 6,377 German keywords with monthly volume, difficulty (KD), topic/subtopic, intent and funnel labels. Visibility: two Google Search Console exports for Germany over a trailing 28-day window. Universe demand matched to GSC by normalised exact query string. Content audit: a supplied list of 219 live DE URLs matched to the GSC landing-page exports (normalised, www/scheme/trailing-slash stripped) to flag pages with no search visibility.

Caveats and assumptions — read before acting

  • Funnel ≈ Intent. The funnel-stage and intent fields are near-identical (Top↔Educational, Bottom↔Transactional). Treat them as one signal, not two.
  • Search demand ≠ revenue. Volume signals need and language, not market size or income. The commercial-sizing tab is an illustrative funnel model with editable placeholders, not a forecast.
  • Estimated volumes. Monthly volumes are third-party estimates; treat sub-50 figures as directional.
  • GSC window is short. 28 days; no seasonality or trend supplied, so growth claims are flagged as hypotheses.
  • Matching is conservative. Exact-string matching under-counts Moss visibility where wording differs; true coverage is somewhat higher than ~1.8%, but the order of magnitude (very low) holds.
  • Visibility ≠ indexing. A page absent from the 28-day GSC export has negligible impressions in that window; it is not proof of de-indexing. Confirm individual URLs against full Search Console coverage and a longer window.
  • Brand handling. The universe contains no Moss-branded terms by design; brand demand is reported separately from GSC.
  • Broad/ambiguous heads. Terms like ‘budget’, ‘rechnung’, and ‘General Finance > Uncategorised’ (438k) are catch-alls that overstate any single coherent opportunity.
  • No CPC / paid data. Commercial-value scoring relies on intent and product fit as proxies; validate against CPC and CRM data.
